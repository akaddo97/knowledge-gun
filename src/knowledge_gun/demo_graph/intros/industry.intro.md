## The space the studio operates in

Lantern-Bough sits in the atmospheric-narrative indie segment — small teams, hand-crafted aesthetics, premium PC pricing, no live-service tail. The 2024 industry-landscape document names roughly forty active studios in this segment globally; perhaps a dozen are recognisable to a typical genre fan. The segment is small but resilient: it has weathered the 2024 platform-revenue compression and the 2023 layoff cycle better than mid-budget studios because its cost base never required hit titles to clear.

## Direct competitors

**Glass Cliff Games.** Mid-size studio, roughly 18 people, working in the same atmospheric-narrative space. Their last release shipped in early 2024 to strong reviews and meaningful commercial numbers. They are the segment's reference point for "scaled atmospheric narrative". Lantern-Bough has chosen explicitly not to scale into Glass Cliff's territory; the studios are friendly and the founders have spoken on the same conference panels.

**Pixel Orchard Studio.** Small studio, roughly 4 people, with a back catalogue of two pixel-art adventure games. Closer in scope and headcount to Lantern-Bough than Glass Cliff. The two studios are direct competitors for storefront attention during seasonal release windows but the work is differentiated enough — Pixel Orchard leans more puzzle-adventure, Lantern-Bough more narrative-environmental — that the overlap on player wishlists is partial rather than full.

The 2024 landscape doc names both Glass Cliff and Pixel Orchard as the studios Lantern-Bough is most often shelved next to in storefront curation, in genre tagging and in editorial roundups. Pricing across all three sits in the same band (roughly €15–20 launch, discounted to €8–12 within twelve months).

## Publishing and distribution

**Lantern Press** is the studio's publishing partner. A boutique indie publisher with a roster of roughly twelve titles, narrative-led PC focus, no platform-holder ties. Lantern-Bough has worked with them on both Tide Études and Paper Lanterns. The relationship is contractually loose — Lantern Press takes a marketing-and-storefront-relations cut and a smaller revenue share than typical mid-tier publishers — and Lantern-Bough retains IP and creative control across the board.

**Hollow Bough Distribution** is the storefront-and-key-distribution layer. They handle the Steam relationship, regional pricing, key generation for press and influencer programmes, and post-launch sale event participation. Both Tide Études and Paper Lanterns ship through them.

## Funding context

**Indie Haven Fund** is the EU-non-profit grant body that funded Cinder Path's 2021 cycle and Tide Études' 2022 cycle at sub-€100k per cycle. The fund's grants are the closest thing in Europe to a no-strings-attached design-stage runway for indie studios in this segment. Lantern-Bough has not taken Indie Haven money since 2022 — Paper Lanterns is self-funded from Tide Études revenue plus a Lantern Press advance — but the studio's relationship with the fund is in good standing and a future application for a fourth project remains a live option.

## Storefront and pricing landscape

The seasonal release cadence (winter sale, summer sale, autumn discount events) shapes when this segment ships rather than how it builds. Lantern-Bough has missed one storefront moment per project to date and has explicitly framed that as an acceptable trade for scope quality. Glass Cliff's 2024 release hit its window; Pixel Orchard's 2023 release missed by six weeks and recovered commercially. The lesson the landscape doc draws is that storefront windows matter less than review reception in this segment; quality is more durable than calendar.

## What the segment is not

It is not the AAA narrative space (those are publisher-funded and operate at 20-50x the budget). It is not the casual-mobile narrative space (those operate at 10-100x the unit volume on free-to-play monetisation). It is not the visual-novel scene proper (the genre overlap is partial, not full). The segment's defensibility comes from the gap between AAA narrative and visual-novel narrative — a gap that requires hand-craft volumes that AAA cannot afford and visual-novel teams cannot produce.
