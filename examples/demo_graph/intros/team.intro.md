## Who the team is

Lantern-Bough is six people. Below is what each of them does, where they sit, and what makes them load-bearing on the current project.

**Maya Okonkwo — founder, creative director.** Started the studio in 2021 after eight years at larger studios in narrative roles. Owns the creative vision across all three projects. Author of the 2024 studio manifesto. Based in Lisbon. Maya holds final say on scope, tone and any feature that touches narrative. She does not write code on shipping projects, but prototypes design ideas in Godot for internal review.

**Jonas Lindqvist — engineering lead.** Joined in 2022 from a mid-size studio. Owns gameplay systems, tooling and the engine integration. Based in Montréal. Jonas's work on Tide Études' procedural-music runtime is what made the project's audio-driven puzzles feel like puzzles rather than ambient noise; the same architectural pattern carries into Paper Lanterns. He is the team's point person on engine choice (Godot since 2022), build pipelines and the modding layer that ships with each title.

**Priya Balasubramanian — art director.** Joined in 2022 from freelance illustration work. Owns the visual language — handpainted pixel art with parallax compositing as the house style. Based in Lisbon. Priya runs a small art pipeline that mixes hand-drawn frames with light procedural variation; the look is not template-driven and cannot be replicated by tooling alone. She is the slowest constraint on most milestones, by design — the team scopes around her capacity rather than against it.

**Diego Alvarez — sound designer and composer.** Joined in 2023 specifically to push the procedural-music thesis from Tide Études further. Based in Kyoto. Diego's adaptive audio work is one of the studio's two strongest critical-reception lines (alongside Priya's art). He composes, records, mixes and writes the runtime audio code. He is the team member with the most overlap with Jonas — adaptive audio is a systems problem before it is an aesthetic one.

**Sarah Chen — narrative designer.** Joined in 2023 to take the writing load off Maya so Maya could direct rather than write. Based in Lisbon. Sarah owns branching dialogue authoring, the worldbuilding bible for Paper Lanterns, and the studio's writing tooling (a small in-house tree editor that exports to Godot resources). The dialogue tree on Paper Lanterns is roughly an order of magnitude larger than anything Tide Études shipped, and Sarah's hire is the reason that scope was even contemplatable.

**Amir Hassan — producer.** Joined in 2024 to take everything that wasn't the work off the team. Based in Montréal. Amir runs schedules, contracts, partner relationships with Lantern Press and Hollow Bough, the Indie Haven grant relationship, and the bi-annual in-person sessions. He is the only person who routinely talks to people outside the studio, which the team treats as both a relief and a single point of failure.

## How the team works

Remote-first, two in-person weeks a year (February planning, September milestone retreat), async-default communication, weekly creative reviews on Fridays where work-in-progress is shared without written context. The team has not crunched on any of three projects to date and treats that as a hard rule rather than a soft preference.
