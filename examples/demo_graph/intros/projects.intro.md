## The three projects

Lantern-Bough has shipped one game, paused one, and is mid-development on a third. Below is each in order of when it began.

### Cinder Path — paused

Started in late 2021 as the studio's first project. A roguelike with hand-drawn pixel art and a procedurally-generated environmental-storytelling layer. Funded by a 2021 Indie Haven grant. Reached a playable prototype after roughly eight months and was paused indefinitely in mid-2022 after the team concluded that the core design loop (procedurally-authored narrative beats integrated with roguelike runs) did not produce coherent player experiences at the resolution they wanted.

The pause memo, drafted in July 2022, names the failure precisely: emergent narrative across short procedurally-generated runs requires either heavy scaffolding (which kills emergence) or an extraordinary content density (which kills the team). The studio chose to learn from the prototype rather than ship a compromised version. Several systems written for Cinder Path — the dialogue runtime, the parallax pixel-art pipeline — carried forward into Tide Études.

### Tide Études — released

Started immediately after the Cinder Path pause in mid-2022. A procedural-music puzzle game where the player solves spatial puzzles by routing musical phrases through a tide-pool environment. Funded by a 2022 Indie Haven grant. Released in 2024 through Lantern Press and Hollow Bough Distribution.

Commercially modest — the title is in the four-figure-units-per-month range as of late 2024 — but covered its development costs and paid for the next eighteen months of studio runway. Critically the strongest reception the studio has had: Diego's adaptive-audio work and Priya's parallax pixel-art language were both called out across reviews. The 2024 postmortem retro names the procedural-music runtime architecture as the single most reusable artefact of the project; that architecture is now the spine of Paper Lanterns' audio system.

### Paper Lanterns — in development

Started March 2023, currently in production. The studio's flagship — atmospheric narrative game built around emergent-storytelling and player-authored-meaning principles drawn straight from the 2024 manifesto. Diegetic UI throughout: the player reads no health bars, no minimaps, no prompt overlays. All five team members other than the founder are working on it; Maya is creative directing.

Self-funded from Tide Études revenue plus a marketing advance from Lantern Press. Distributed by Hollow Bough Distribution on launch. Scoped to roughly a year of remaining work as of early 2025. Key technical risks named in the design doc: the dialogue tree (roughly 10x the size of Tide Études' branching layer, owned by Sarah Chen with tooling support from Jonas Lindqvist), and the parallax-art pipeline (Priya's capacity is the binding constraint on the milestone schedule).

## What the projects share

A house style that is recognisable across all three — handpainted pixel art with parallax compositing, adaptive audio that responds to gameplay state, narrative as system rather than script. The studio treats this style as the moat. Reskinning would take a competitor several years to replicate, and the team's view is that no technology has shortened that gap meaningfully.

## What the projects deliberately avoid

Multiplayer. Mobile. Free-to-play. Live service. Any monetisation pattern that pressures pacing decisions. Any scope that requires a headcount above seven.
